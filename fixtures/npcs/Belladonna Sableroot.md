# Belladonna Sableroot

## Quick ID
**One-liner:** A florist-herbalist whose arrangements are gorgeous, whose ledgers are immaculate, and whose idea of problem-solving is alarmingly scalable.  
**First impression:** Beautifully composed woman with excellent posture and the gaze of someone already assessing poison tolerance.  
**Voice:** Low, precise, unhurried.  
**Tell:** Goes still when making a decision that has no easy undoing.  

## Look
- Silhouette: Tall, poised, rooted stance with very little wasted movement.
- Face / hair / eyes: Calm expression, observant eyes, elegance sharpened by fatigue she refuses to display.
- Clothing / motifs: Dark florist aprons, fitted work sleeves, mourning-silk ribbons, subtle thorn motifs.
- Scent / aura / sound: Damp soil, bitter herbs, polished steel, and expensive funeral flowers.
- Notable item: Florist shears worn like sacred tools; narrow blade hidden where only she can reach it quickly.

## Social Presence
- Public mask: Impeccable florist with a gift for symbolism, etiquette, and discretion.
- Private self: Strategic survivor who understands that beauty lowers defenses.
- What they want from strangers: Clarity, usefulness, and a reason not to dismiss her as decorative.
- What they fear people learning: How much she has turned care, ritual, and aesthetics into weapons.

## Personality Pins
- Core trait: Control
- Contradiction: Deeply caring nature expressed through strict competence and sharp boundaries.
- Soft spot: [[Pip Foxglove]]
- Pet peeve: Sloppiness, condescension, and clients who think discretion includes underpaying.
- Under stress they: Gets quieter, colder, and more exact.

## Motivations
- Goal (current): Keep [[Foxglove & Sableroot Floral]] profitable while filtering side work hard enough to keep them alive.
- Goal (long-term): Build a future where she and Pip can choose beauty without blood attached to it.
- Need (emotional): Stability that cannot be bought out from under her.
- Line they won’t cross: Harming children, desecration, and cruelty for spectacle.
- Line they already crossed: Using her plantcraft and ritual knowledge for intimidation and assassination.

## Relationships
### Allies / Contacts
- [[Pip Foxglove]]
- Outer Ring herbalists, ribbon sellers, shrine tenders, and funerary workers
- select attendants who prefer their deliveries discreet and unremarked upon

### Rivals / Threats
- Brokers who assume florists are easy to squeeze
- wealthy clients who mistake polish for obedience

### Entanglements
- [[Foxglove & Sableroot Floral]]
- courier traffic between rings
- quiet overlap with assassin networks and revolutionary message channels

## Knowledge Scope
**They know (reliably):**
- Floral language, mourning customs, scent masking, and how to read a client from what they order
- Preservation, tinctures, sedatives, irritants, and low-profile poisons
- Which households are grieving, hiding, lying, or trying to say something without using words

**They believe (maybe wrong):**
- Most systems of power only value labor when they can hide the hands performing it
- Pip would eventually die without her planning ahead of him

**They hide (actively):**
- The full extent of her poison work
- How much of her long-term planning is built around worst-case scenarios where Pip does not come home

**They will say if pressed:**
- Which compounds were used in a given job
- Whether a contract smelled political, personal, or panicked
- That she liked the party better before they became recurring administrative trouble

## Secrets
- Secret 1: Funeral-lineage training
  - Proof / tell: Exceptional skill with mourning rituals, memorial arrangements, preservation, and scent control.
  - Who suspects: Other ritual workers and anyone who has seen her do funeral prep.
  - If revealed: Adds weight to the way she handles grief scenes and memorial offerings.

- Secret 2: Will kill to protect Pip without hesitation
  - Proof / tell: Every plan shifts the instant he is in real danger.
  - Who suspects: [[Pip Foxglove]] and anyone with eyes.
  - If revealed: Makes her frighteningly predictable in exactly one direction.

## Clues & Use at the Table
**Clues they can deliver:**
- Which flowers, scents, and arrangements connect to apology, threat, seduction, mourning, and coded warning
- Which clients recently ordered something emotionally strange for the occasion
- How quiet messages and concealed objects can move inside ordinary delivery work

**Clues they can obstruct:**
- Can package evidence, conceal residue, and build plausible deniability through ritual aesthetics
- Speaks plainly enough that lies sound like professional statements

**Three questions to ask them in play:**
1. What does this arrangement actually mean?
2. Who orders flowers like this?
3. What kind of job was this really supposed to be?

## Schedule
**Dawn:** Stem conditioning, herb drying, ledger check, first prep of the day.  
**Morning:** Counter work, bespoke orders, mourning consultations, client reading.  
**Midday:** Back-room assembly, coded packaging, discreet pick-up handoff.  
**Afternoon:** Tincture work, poison prep, preservation, and side-contract evaluation.  
**Evening:** Shop close, accounts, shared meal with Pip, route review, possible contract deployment.  
**Night:** Quiet workroom labor or highly controlled field operations.  

**Variations:** Retreats fully into the back room when stressed or planning something dangerous.  
**Route notes:** Prefers predictable paths, interior shortcuts, and spaces she has already assessed.  
**Places they avoid:** Loud taverns, chaotic rallies, and any employer’s house that requires trust without collateral.  

## Resources
- Money: Shop income plus carefully brokered contract pay.
- Authority: Strong informal respect among ritual workers and select attendants.
- Magic: Minor fae plant attunement and botanical sensitivity.
- People: Herbal suppliers, shrine workers, undertakers, quiet clients, and Pip’s route network.
- Access: Back rooms, mourning spaces, service entrances, and discreet upper-ring delivery access.
