# Pip Foxglove

## Quick ID
**One-liner:** A fast-talking florist-courier who treats lethal work like a timing problem and charm like a survival tool.  
**First impression:** Friendly, impossible to shut up, suspiciously light on his feet.  
**Voice:** Flirtatious, playful, shameless.  
**Tell:** Keeps talking through stealth, injury, panic, and combat alike.  

## Look
- Silhouette: Lean, spring-loaded, always angled like he might vault a table.
- Face / hair / eyes: Bright-eyed, expressive, quick smile that reads harmless until it doesn’t.
- Clothing / motifs: Practical florist layers cut for movement; delivery satchel; hidden sheathes; flower-threaded details.
- Scent / aura / sound: Fresh stems, rain on stone, and trouble approaching at speed.
- Notable item: Folding florist knife that doubles as a killing blade.

## Social Presence
- Public mask: Charming delivery boy turned beloved local florist.
- Private self: Hyper-alert survivor who turns fear into motion and chatter.
- What they want from strangers: A read, an angle, a reason to keep the exchange light.
- What they fear people learning: How much of his ease is performance built over old hunger and instability.

## Personality Pins
- Core trait: Quickness
- Contradiction: Light, funny presence paired with extremely sharp situational awareness.
- Soft spot: [[Belladonna Sableroot]]
- Pet peeve: Being cornered, patronized, or underestimated.
- Under stress they: Gets louder, faster, funnier, and more dangerous.

## Motivations
- Goal (current): Keep [[Foxglove & Sableroot Floral]] solvent while navigating increasingly dangerous side work.
- Goal (long-term): Build a life stable enough that he and Belladonna never have to say yes to the wrong contract again.
- Need (emotional): Security he can trust to last.
- Line they won’t cross: Harming children or taking cruelty jobs.
- Line they already crossed: Killing for money once survival made the first compromise feel necessary.

## Relationships
### Allies / Contacts
- [[Belladonna Sableroot]]
- Outer Ring market sellers
- select attendants, couriers, and broker intermediaries

### Rivals / Threats
- Anyone who tries to hire one without the other
- debtors, failed clients, and underworld brokers who treat florists like disposable assets

### Entanglements
- [[Foxglove & Sableroot Floral]]
- the city’s courier network
- quiet overlap with assassin routes and revolutionary rumor traffic

## Knowledge Scope
**They know (reliably):**
- Rooflines, service routes, delivery corridors, and how messages move between rings
- Which nobles send apology flowers, funeral flowers, and threats disguised as gifts
- Which brokers are safe, which are desperate, and which are planning to burn their assets

**They believe (maybe wrong):**
- Every powerful person eventually chooses convenience over loyalty
- Belladonna is the only truly safe thing in his life

**They hide (actively):**
- How unstable his childhood was
- How much Belladonna’s opinion governs nearly every major choice he makes

**They will say if pressed:**
- Which intermediary handed over a contract
- Whether a job was meant to kill, delay, scare, or retrieve
- That he likes the party more than is professionally wise

## Secrets
- Secret 1: Lane-child survival history
  - Proof / tell: Knows every roofline, hungry habit, and market escape route in his district.
  - Who suspects: Belladonna and anyone else who grew up poor.
  - If revealed: Reframes his charm as a survival tool instead of natural ease.

- Secret 2: Will break for Belladonna faster than for himself
  - Proof / tell: Any threat to her changes his priorities instantly.
  - Who suspects: [[Belladonna Sableroot]] and anyone who watches them for more than ten minutes.
  - If revealed: Makes him emotionally targetable.

## Clues & Use at the Table
**Clues they can deliver:**
- How courier traffic and discreet deliveries actually move through the city
- Which clients use flowers to say things they do not want written down
- Which recent contracts smelled political instead of personal

**Clues they can obstruct:**
- Can bluff, distract, and reframe a scene long enough for Belladonna to reposition
- Will mix truth and jokes until it becomes hard to tell which is which

**Three questions to ask them in play:**
1. Who paid you?
2. Was this a kill job or a pressure job?
3. What were you told about us before we met?

## Schedule
**Dawn:** Market pickup, crate hauling, rooftop shortcuts, first deliveries.  
**Morning:** Front counter charm and rush bouquet runs.  
**Midday:** Long-route deliveries between service corridors and upper districts.  
**Afternoon:** Off-books courier work, message drops, and scouting for side contracts.  
**Evening:** Shop closeout, rooftop air, shared dinner with Belladonna, occasional contract briefing.  
**Night:** Ambush work, retrieval jobs, or sleeping lightly with a knife near the bed.  

**Variations:** Disappears from the shop when a contract requires mobility or surveillance.  
**Route notes:** Prefers roofs, balconies, laundry lines, and side stairs over streets.  
**Places they avoid:** Formal physician wards, heavily warded palace interiors, and any room with only one exit.  

## Resources
- Money: Modest shop income plus irregular contract pay.
- Authority: None officially; strong informal favor network in the Outer Ring.
- Magic: Minor fae agility and movement tricks.
- People: Market sellers, attendants, messengers, low-level fixers.
- Access: Delivery routes, rooftop paths, service entrances, neighborhoods Belladonna cannot reach as quickly.
